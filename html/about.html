About

"What Can I See Tonight?" is a site for amateur astronomers. More specifically, "What Can I See Tonight? focuses on the practical needs and questions of someone starting out - in other words, you own a telescope or set of binoculars, but you're not entirely sure what to do with it.

The main page shows you what viewing conditions to expect for your zip code (including weather conditions, % humidity, and the current moon phase). In turn, the blog offers astronomy tips and the Resources page lists other useful web sites and iOS/Android apps.

"What Can I See Tonight?" is produced by Jen Stevens, who first started doing observational astronomy when she lived in an area with relatively low light pollution. She now lives in an area with high light pollution.

The header image, which features the Virgo Galaxy Cluster, is courtesy of NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory.
