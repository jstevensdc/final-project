Why Does Humidity Matter?

Humidity matters because it can affect how bright the sky is - when the humidity is high enough, individual bits of air vapor (aerosols) literally scatter light across the sky, creating a somewhat milky effect and excerbating the effects of light pollution. High humidity can also cause water condensation on lenses and other telescope parts.

However, high humidity can also be helpful - for instance, it can help decrease the "twinkling" effect often observed because of the refraction of star light through layers of air.
